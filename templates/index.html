About Kismet Combined School

1.2. INTRODUCTION

Kismet Combined School was founded in 1984. The present buildings were completed in 1983 and additions in 1993. The name kismet is derived from its situation in Kismet Park. The word 'KISMET' signifies "DESTINY" or "FATE". Like a flower or a tree to develop to its full potential will depend on how well it will be nurtured. The realisation of this potentiality of the flower is called "Kismet". Likewise, our school's potential or Kismet will depend on how well it is cared for. Let's hope we live up to our name: - TO CARE – TO STRIVE – TO ACHIEVE THE BEST FOR OUR CHILDREN

1.3. MISSION

We at Kismet Combined school, wish to provide excellent education in the community we serve, through the knowledge, skills and attitudes of a suitable staff, who strive to develop the intellectual, physical and spiritual facilities of the learners to help them achieve excellence. We do this in the belief that discipline, trust, tolerance and participation of all stakeholders are essential to our goal of moulding – enlightened, caring and responsible individuals.

1.4. VISION

To be the best school in the North West, by providing the best quality education and imparting knowledge in an effective and efficient manner.

1.5. AIM

Effective discipline is directly dependent upon the limits set by a Code of acceptable behaviour. The School's Code of Conduct is based on fostering a culture of mutual respect in an orderly, clean and safe environment. The school rules are straightforward and we expect both parents and children to be familiar with them. We aim at establishing a disciplined and purposeful environment to facilitate effective education and learning.

This Code of Conduct services as a general guide for the standards of dress and behaviour expected of Kismet Secondary School Learners. Each Learners are expected not only to conform to these standards, but also to assist the staff in ensuring that all learners conform to them. Learners are expected at all rimes to be loyal and to uphold the honour of our school.
